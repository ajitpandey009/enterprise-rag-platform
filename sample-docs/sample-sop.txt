Standard Operating Procedure: Incident Response
=================================================

Document ID: SOP-INC-001
Version: 2.3
Last Updated: 2024-12-01
Owner: Platform Engineering Team

1. PURPOSE
This SOP defines the procedures for handling production incidents
across all enterprise systems, ensuring consistent response and
minimal business impact.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all P1-P4 incidents across:
- Core banking platform
- Customer-facing applications
- Internal tooling and dashboards
- Data pipeline systems

3. SEVERITY LEVELS

P1 - Critical:
- Complete service outage
- Data loss or corruption
- Security breach
- Response time: 15 minutes
- Resolution target: 1 hour

P2 - High:
- Major feature degradation
- Performance issues affecting >50% users
- Response time: 30 minutes
- Resolution target: 4 hours

P3 - Medium:
- Minor feature issues
- Workaround available
- Response time: 2 hours
- Resolution target: 24 hours

P4 - Low:
- Cosmetic issues
- Documentation errors
- Response time: 24 hours
- Resolution target: 5 business days

4. INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURE

Step 1: Detection and Triage
- Monitor alerts from PagerDuty/Grafana
- Acknowledge the incident within the response time
- Assess severity level based on criteria above
- Assign an Incident Commander (IC)

Step 2: Communication
- Create incident channel in Slack (#inc-YYYYMMDD-description)
- Notify stakeholders based on severity level
- Post initial status update within 15 minutes

Step 3: Investigation
- Gather logs from ELK Stack
- Check metrics in Grafana dashboards
- Review recent deployments in ArgoCD
- Analyze error patterns in Sentry

Step 4: Resolution
- Implement fix or rollback
- Verify fix in staging environment
- Deploy to production
- Monitor for 30 minutes post-deployment

Step 5: Post-Incident
- Schedule post-mortem within 48 hours
- Document root cause analysis
- Create action items for prevention
- Update runbooks as needed

5. ESCALATION MATRIX
- Engineering Lead: After 30 minutes without progress
- VP Engineering: P1 incidents lasting > 1 hour
- CTO: P1 incidents lasting > 2 hours
- CEO: P1 incidents with customer data impact
